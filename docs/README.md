# OctoAcme Project Management Process Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme project management process documentation! This folder is the central hub for understanding how OctoAcme plans, executes, and continuously improves its software delivery. Whether you are a new teammate onboarding to the team, a contributor looking for process guidance, or a stakeholder seeking to understand our delivery approach, you will find everything you need here.

## Summary of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme follows a structured, lifecycle-based approach to project delivery built around five phases: **Initiation → Planning → Execution → Release → Retrospective**. Each phase has clear entry and exit criteria, defined owners, and purpose-built artifacts. During Initiation, the team aligns on the problem statement, identifies stakeholders, and establishes a high-level timeline. Planning translates that alignment into a concrete roadmap, sprint backlog, resource plan, and dependency map. Execution drives iterative, testable delivery with twice-weekly standups, continuous integration, and rolling acceptance testing. Release coordinates deployment, verification, and stakeholder announcement. Finally, the Retrospective phase captures learnings and feeds improvements back into the next cycle.

The processes are grounded in five core principles: **customer-first** prioritization that keeps usability and value at the center of every decision; **iterative delivery** through small, testable increments that reduce risk and accelerate feedback; **clear ownership** so that every project has a named Project Manager and Product Lead accountable for outcomes; **data-informed decisions** backed by metrics and evidence rather than assumption; and **psychological safety** that encourages candid feedback, experimentation, and learning without blame.

Four core roles collaborate to move work through the lifecycle. The **Project Manager (PM)** coordinates delivery, schedules, risk, and cross-team communications. The **Product Manager (PdM)** defines outcomes, prioritizes the backlog, and measures success against customer and business goals. **Developers** implement features, participate in design and code reviews, and maintain test coverage and observability. **QA/Testing** validates quality and ensures acceptance criteria are met before each release. Stakeholders provide inputs and approvals at key milestones. Communication follows a predictable cadence—weekly PM/PdM syncs, twice-weekly delivery standups, monthly stakeholder updates, and ad-hoc escalations when risks materialize.

Risk management is continuous and transparent. Risks are captured in a living Risk Register with owners, likelihood, impact scores, and mitigation plans. Escalation paths are defined in advance so that blockers surface quickly and decisions reach the right people without delay. Quality assurance is baked into every sprint through unit, integration, and end-to-end testing strategies, with the Definition of Done enforced at the story level. Retrospectives close each cycle by celebrating wins, identifying friction points, and producing concrete action items that make the next cycle better—ensuring OctoAcme's delivery capability improves continuously over time.

## How to Use These Docs

- **New teammates**: Start with the [Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md) to understand the big picture, then read [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) to see where you fit in.
- **Project Managers and Product Managers**: Use [Project Initiation Guide](octoacme-project-initiation.md) and [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md) at the start of each engagement, and refer to [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) throughout delivery.
- **Developers and QA**: Focus on [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) for day-to-day guidance and [Release & Deployment Guide](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) when preparing a release.
- **Stakeholders**: The [Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md) and [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) docs provide the context most relevant to your involvement.
- **Everyone**: Review [Retrospective & Continuous Improvement](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md) after each project cycle to contribute to and benefit from shared learnings.

> **Tip:** Add the docs in this folder to your Copilot Space context (via `.copilot/`) so that GitHub Copilot can provide role-specific, process-aware guidance as you work.
