# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Risk Managers

### Role Summary
Risk Managers own the risk register, lead risk reviews, and ensure mitigation steps are followed through. They partner with Project Managers and Product Managers to surface critical risks and escalate when needed.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and update the Risk Register throughout the project lifecycle
- Facilitate risk identification sessions during planning and execution
- Lead risk review discussions during weekly syncs
- Track mitigation strategy implementation and follow-up actions
- Escalate high-impact risks to sponsors and stakeholders
- Capture post-incident lessons learned and update risk profiles

### Goals
- Reduce delivery disruption from avoidable risks
- Keep risk ownership and mitigation progress transparent
- Improve team preparedness through proactive risk management

### Typical Communication
- Weekly risk reviews with Project Managers and Product Managers
- Mitigation follow-ups with Developers and dependent teams
- Escalation summaries for sponsors and key stakeholders

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for user flows, prototypes, design QA, and usability validation. They collaborate with Product Managers and Developers so that delivered features meet usability expectations and accessibility goals.

### Responsibilities
- Create and iterate user flows and wireframes from requirements
- Develop prototypes and mockups for feedback and validation
- Conduct design reviews and usability testing
- Define design QA criteria for feature acceptance
- Partner on accessibility and design system alignment
- Incorporate user research and feedback into feature definitions

### Goals
- Improve usability and task success for target users
- Reduce rework caused by late-stage design issues
- Keep product experiences consistent and accessible

### Typical Communication
- Discovery and scope alignment with Product Managers
- Design walkthroughs and implementation pairing with Developers
- Usability findings and design QA outcomes shared with Project Managers

---

## Release Coordinators

### Role Summary
Release Coordinators manage the release calendar, ensure deployment checklists are followed, and coordinate release communications. They work closely with Developers, Product Managers, and Support during release windows.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule release windows and maintenance downtime
- Confirm pre-release requirements are met (testing, security scans, release notes)
- Execute deployment checklists and verify post-deployment health
- Coordinate release communications with Support, Sales, and Customer Success
- Manage rollback procedures when release issues arise
- Document release outcomes and improvement opportunities

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize customer-facing disruption during deployments
- Improve release quality and team confidence over time

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness checkpoints with Project Managers and Developers
- Go/no-go coordination with Product Managers and Support
- Release status updates and post-release summaries for stakeholders

---

## Customer Success Leads

### Role Summary
Customer Success Leads guide onboarding, capture early user feedback, and coordinate post-release handoffs. They partner with Product Managers and Project Managers to track adoption outcomes and inform improvement loops.

### Responsibilities
- Design and run customer onboarding plans
- Capture early user feedback and adoption metrics
- Identify gaps between expected and actual usage patterns
- Coordinate handoffs between engineering, product, and support teams
- Track and communicate customer satisfaction and success metrics
- Inform roadmap prioritization based on customer insights

### Goals
- Accelerate time-to-value for new releases
- Improve retention and customer satisfaction outcomes
- Turn customer signals into actionable delivery improvements

### Typical Communication
- Onboarding plans and adoption updates with Product Managers
- Usage feedback loops with Developers and support teams
- Cross-functional handoff coordination with Project Managers
